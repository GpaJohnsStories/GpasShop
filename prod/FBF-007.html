Adventurous African Tales

Kevin G Frohlich
FBF-007

If you’ve got a child who loves animals or a grandchild who’s full of questions about the wild places of the world, this book is a real treat.

Each story takes you onto the African savanna, where the animals act with the same quirks and personalities the author watched as a boy growing up near the Zambezi River.

The pen‑and‑ink drawings give each creature its own character, and the tales are simple enough for kids to follow but full of the kind of wisdom they’ll carry with them.

The author writes from real memories — camping with his grandpa, watching wildlife along the riverbanks, and dreaming of being a game ranger. Those early days shaped these stories, and you can feel that love for Africa on every page.

The animals may be fictional, but they’re drawn and described in ways that match their true nature and environment.

It’s a fun book to read aloud, and a good one for helping children imagine, wonder, and grow.

These stories carry simple, child‑friendly morals — some spoken in faith, others in everyday wisdom — which is why I’ve tucked this little book under “faith” in my shop.

This is one of those books that stays on my desk because the stories and drawings make me smile every time I open it.
