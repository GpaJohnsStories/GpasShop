Father and I Were Ranchers: Little Britches, Book 1

Ralph Moody
HBK-001

This is the kind of book that settles into a family and stays there.

Ralph Moody was only eight when his family moved from New Hampshire to a ranch southwest of Denver, Colorado — land that is now part of a town — but in these pages it’s still wide‑open country, full of hard work, grit, and the kind of growing‑up lessons that shape a life.

I remember my mother reading this book to me once or twice when I was young, and years later my wife read it to our kids and then later to some of our grandkids.

The story has that rare mix of honesty and adventure that keeps children listening and adults nodding along.

This is the first book in Moody’s series, but it stands strong on its own. It’s a fine read‑aloud and a good reminder of courage, responsibility, and the quiet strength of family.
